Figure 1: Tree Map

Figure 1 shows a tree map data visualization of orchestra instruments. Each category of orchestra instruments (i.e., strings, percussion, woodwinds) is assigned a rectangle area with subcategory rectangles of instrument counts in each category. These are further grouped by orchestra type (i.e., modern, early romantic, classical). By looking at this data visualization, it's easy to see how string instruments tend to make up the vast majority of orchestras with similar proportions of percussion, woodwinds, and keyboards seen throughout.



Figure 2: Bump Chart

Figure 2 shows a bump chart data visualization of alien species record counts by continent. The vertical thickness of the line as you go across years corresponds to the magnitude of alien species record counts. By looking at this, it appears that Europe has a significantly higher magnitude of alien record counts when compared to places such as Asia or Africa.



Figure 3: Bubble Chart

Figure 3 shows a data visualization depicting data from GoodReads on the top ten according to number of ratings. A circle packing chart was used to visualize the different books along with the author, average rating, and total number of ratings dispalyed in each circle. The size of the circles corresponds to the number of ratings, with colors corresponding to the book title (see legend on right). By looking at this, it's apparent that Stephanie Meyer's Twilight has the most ratings for books on GoodReads while J.K. Rowling's Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban has the highest average rating. Data Source: https://www.kaggle.com/jealousleopard/goodreadsbook.
